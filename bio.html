UAVs for custom data collection The cutting edge of remote sensing

As a Geospatial Research Specialist at Northern Arizona's Remote Sensing and Geoinformatics Lab, one of my prime responsibilities was managing two Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) platforms.

I was responsible for craft maintenence, flight planning, piloting (and landing!), and data processing following missions. The octocopter pictured here weighs about 25 pounds and is equipped with a GNSS GPS, a lidar scanner and a hyperspectral sensor.

I successfully lead data collection missions to assess sand dune volume on the Paria Plateau, to measure Ponderosa Pine forest metrics near Flagstaff, AZ, and in partnership with Arizona Department of Natural Resources near Tombstone, AZ.

Post-fire sediment yield modeling At the watershed scale

As a GIS contractor for the USGS I worked on a post-fire sediment yield modeling project. To do this we used the InVEST Natural Capital modeling tool. I was responsible for collecting all the relevant input datasets, troubleshooting and running the models.

I ran these models for every HUC8 watershed in the western United States at pre and post-fire scenarios. This was then combined with Global Circulation Model (GCM) fire area projections to generate decadal maps of watershed area burned and sediment prodced.

This was the first landscape-scale modeling project to combine post-fire sediment yield estimates with future fire scenarios. The manuscript is currently under review with the journal Nature Geosciences.
